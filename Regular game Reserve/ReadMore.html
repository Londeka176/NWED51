The heart of the reserve

If you are willing, walk with me. The sun is just beginning to rise over the grasslands, and the air is cool enough to carry the scent of wild sage. Do you hear that? The low, distant rumble, which is a herd of buffalo moving toward the river. This is where our story begins. The reserve was not always this alive. Over years ago, the land was bare in places, the animals scattered by hunters and fences. But in the early 2010s, while others were furious about the arrival of a soccer world-cup, a small group of Drakensberg locals decided to protect what was left. They did not have much, just a few tools, a patch of land, and a stubborn belief that the wild could return. As we walk, imagine the decades passing. Grass creeping back over the earth. Herds returning, first shy antelope, then elephants, then the deep, commanding roar of lions. I remember the day the first rhinos came back, how the ground seemed to tremble under their weight, and how we all stood silent, as if afraid to break the moment. Look there, beyond the acacia trees. That is where Foreigners gather on field trips, their voices hushed when they spot a giraffe bending to drink. I have seen their faces, wide eyes, mouths opened in wonder, and I think of the people who started all this, who dreamed of a place where the wild could breathe again. Now, as we reach the edge of the river, I'll tell you the truth: the reserve's story is not about animals or land. It is about the people who refused to let beauty vanish. And if you listen closely, you can still hear their hope in the wind, carrying the promise that this story will never truly end.

The History of The Game Reserve

Regular Game Reserve was founded in the early 2010s, by the small group of Drakensberg locals. They had extremely big stubbornness about wildlife, and they really fought to prevent the beauty of nature from vanishing. After a decade of successful struggle, they really brought it together. They gathered, protected, and maintained wildlife in an attractive and educative manner. Regular, “which is something happening at a fixed consistent interval.” They named the Regular Game Reserve, which confirms the standard rules, patterns and most importantly the goal of passing the beauty of wildlife from generation to generation.
